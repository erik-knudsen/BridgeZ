Defense Opening Leads and Signals.

Opening Leads.

Top of a Non Honour Suit.
Play the top of a non honour suit.

Top of a Doubleton.
Play the top of a doubleton (either honour or non-honour card), obviously followed by the lower card.

Honour Suit With No Honour Sequence.
Choose 4th best lead, 3rd best from 4 or 5-card suits and 5th best from longer suits or low encouraging which means a low card encourages play in the suit. See Leads in the Bid and Play Configuration Dialogs.

Ace, King Sequence.
Choose which card to lead. See Leads in the Bid and Play Configuration Dialogs.

Non Honour 3 Card Suit.
Choose high, low or Middle Up Down (MUD). See Leads in the Bid and Play Configuration Dialogs.

Leads Against SUIT Contracts.
From any honour sequence always lead the top honour. From an interior honour sequence lead the top of the internal honour sequence - this does include the ‘10’. Never underlead Ace.

Leads Against NO-TRUMP Contracts
From any three card honour sequence always lead the highest card. From an interior honour sequence lead the top of the internal honour sequence - this does include the ‘10’. Ace can be underleaded.

Signals.

When following to a suit or when discarding in a suit, the relative spot of a card is used to send signals. Signals are usually given with cards from the Two spot to the Nine spot.

Attitude Signal
The attitude signal indicates the level of interest toward the suit, in which the signal is given. Common practice include positive and negative attitude signals. A positive signal can be either a relative high card or a relative low card. And a negative signal the opposite. See Signals in the Bid and Play Configuration Dialogs.

1. A positive signal shows positive attitude toward the suit being led.
2. A negative signal shows negative attitude toward the suit being led.


Count Signal
The count signal is used to show the number of cards held in a suit being led. Common practice include even and uneven signals. An even signal can either be a relative high card or a relative low card. And an uneven signal the opposite. See Signals in the Bid and Play Configuration Dialogs.
1. An even signal shows an even number of cards in a suit being led.
2. An uneven signal shows an odd number of cards in a suit being led.
