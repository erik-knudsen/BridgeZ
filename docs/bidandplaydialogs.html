Bid and Play Configuration Dialogs.

The following dialogs determines bid and play conventions.


The above dialog allows you to choose which groups of conventions should be used for the two bridge pairs. They do not necessarily have to be the same. It also has choices so you can define a new group of conventions, edit the currently selected group of conventions or to delete the currently selected group of conventions.



When you are going to define a new group of conventions you must give it a name and you can base the new group on a reference group.


The following dialogs define the components of bid and play conventions.

Config.

Here you can define how aggressive the computer should bid in the auction phase. This functionality is not implemented yet.


General.

This dialog defines general conventions. What style to use? Do you open with 4 or 5 card majors? How about slam conventions? Do you use Stayman, and how? Do you use Jacoby transfers? And other miscellaneous stuff. Note that some of the choices are dependant on other choices (mostly in this dialog). If a choice is predetermined then the computer will not allow you to change it.


2-Bids.

Here we define how 2 bid open is handled. Do you use weak two bid open? What are the parameters of strong 2C opening?


NT Range.

This dialog defines opening NT range, and whether you allow a weak 5 card major when you open 1NT,


NT Other.

There are a number of other NT related bid conventions which are determined in the above dialog.


Misc.

A number of miscellaneous conventions are defined by this dialog. Advanced conventions? Are jump overcalls weak and does 2NT response have a special meaning? What is the nature of three level preempts?


Opening.

What are the requirements for opening 1 Bid? This is not implemented.


Leads

Opening leads are defined in this dialog. Leads are defined related to pair and suit/NT. You pick your choices in the dialog. The computer does not itself use this information, but it does understand it and it does play according to it. The computer determines play by double dummy algorithms. Among the equal valid plays determined by the double dummy algorithm opening play is chosen in accordance with the choices selected in this dialog.


Signals.

Signals are defined by this dialog. The computer does not itself use this information, but it does understand it and it does play according to it. The computer determines play by double dummy algorithms. Among the equal valid plays determined by the double dummy algorithm play is chosen in accordance with the choices selected in this dialog.
